## WHAT HAPPENS AFTER FILING

### Phase 1: Receipt & Acknowledgment (1-2 weeks)
- Patent Office issues filing receipt with application number
- Application enters examination queue
- Your details added to patent registry

### Phase 2: Formality Examination (3-6 months)
- Patent Office checks formal compliance
- Verifies documents are complete and legible
- Checks fee payment
- May issue minor objections (e.g., "document unclear")
- You respond to formality issues

### Phase 3: Substantive Examination (6-24 months)
- Patent Examiner reviews specification against prior art
- Searches patents/publications for novelty
- Issues examination report with objections/acceptance
- Common grounds for objection:
  - Lack of novelty (something similar exists)
  - Lack of inventive step (obvious to person skilled in art)
  - Insufficient disclosure
  - Unclear claims

### Phase 4: Response & Prosecution (Ongoing)
- You respond to Examiner's objections
- Amendments: modify claims to overcome objections
- Arguments: provide evidence/reasoning why claims are novel
- Multiple rounds possible until acceptance

### Phase 5: Grant & Publication
- Patent issued upon final acceptance
- Published in Patent Office Gazette
- Patent term: 20 years from filing date (09-04-2026 to 09-04-2046)

1. **Independent claims** are broad and catch variations
2. **Dependent claims** add specificity while preserving base coverage
3. **Method claims** capture different implementations

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Q: Can I file this myself or do I need a patent attorney?
**A:** You can file yourself (pro-se) to save costs. However, a patent attorney can:
- Strengthen claims for better coverage
- Help respond to examiner objections
- Increase chances of grant
- Cost: ₹50,000-200,000 for full prosecution

**Recommendation:** File yourself initially (you've already done the hard work), then engage attorney if examiner raises objections.

### Q: What if someone sues me for infringement before I get a patent?
**A:** Patents only protect you AFTER grant. You cannot sue for infringement during prosecution. Keep the filing date (09-04-2026) confidential until patent is published (18 months after filing).

### Q: Can I still sell/commercialize while application is pending?
**A:** Yes. Patent pending status gives you legal standing to collect damages if patent grants and infringement occurred during pending period.

### Q: What if someone else files a similar patent?
**A:** Your filing date (09-04-2026) is your priority date. Earlier filing date = earlier priority = you get the patent if your claims are broader.

### Q: How long until patent is granted?
**A:** Typical timeline:
- Provisional filing: 09-04-2026
- Full application: 09-04-2027
- Examination report: ~09-10-2028
- Grant decision: ~09-10-2028 to 09-04-2029

Total: ~24-36 months from initial filing

### Q: Can I modify claims later?
**A:** Yes, you can amend claims during prosecution to overcome objections, but:
- Amendments cannot add NEW subject matter
- Amendments must be supported by original specification
- Some examiners restrict amendments

**Recommendation:** Keep original specification broad enough to support claim variations.
